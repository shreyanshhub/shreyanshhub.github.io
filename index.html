Shreyansh Pathak

I study how machine-learning systems can selectively forget information while preserving the knowledge that should remain.

I am a Ph.D. student in artificial intelligence at the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur, advised by Prof. Richa Singh and Prof. Mayank Vatsa. My research spans machine unlearning, generative AI, privacy-preserving learning, computer vision, and audio biometrics. I am particularly interested in the mathematical structure of learned representations and in practical, verifiable ways to remove information from trained models.

01

Research

My work asks a simple but difficult question: after a model has learned from data, how can we make it reliably forget a person, class, concept, or attribute without retraining from scratch and without damaging unrelated capabilities?

I explore this problem across text-to-image generation, vision classification, speech, and biometric systems. Current interests include representation geometry, efficient unlearning, multimodal evaluation, robustness against relearning and adversarial prompts, and privacy–utility trade-offs.

  • Machine unlearning
  • Generative AI
  • Privacy-preserving machine learning
  • Deep learning and representation geometry
  • Computer vision and audio biometrics
  • Responsible and trustworthy AI
